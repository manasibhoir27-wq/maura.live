Our STEM Activities

Science Explorers

  • Biology Discovery (microscopes, plant/insect study)
  • Weather & Climate Science

Tech Wizards

Coding, robotics, and digital creation in an outdoor setting.

  • Beginner Robotics & Coding (Scratch, block-based)

Engineering Builders

STEM Curriculum Framework

Our curriculum is designed by educators and STEM professionals to align with educational standards while maintaining the fun of camp.

Age-Appropriate Learning Paths

6-8

Explorers (Ages 6-8)

Introduction to STEM through play, simple experiments, and nature discovery. Focus on observation, asking questions, and basic problem-solving.

9-11

Inventors (Ages 9-11)

Hands-on projects, team challenges, and intermediate STEM concepts. Developing critical thinking and collaborative skills.

12-14

Innovators (Ages 12-14)

Advanced projects, real-world applications, and mentorship opportunities. Focus on design thinking and solution development.

Daily Camp Structure

Morning Science Circle (9:00 AM)

Theme introduction, safety briefing, and daily goals

STEM Lab Session (9:30 AM - 12:00 PM)

Hands-on experiments, project work, and guided discovery

Lunch & Science Talk (12:00 - 1:00 PM)

Nutrition break with guest speakers or science demonstrations

Adventure STEM (1:00 - 3:30 PM)

Outdoor exploration, field studies, and applied learning

Showcase & Reflection (3:30 - 4:00 PM)

Present projects, share discoveries, and daily wrap-up

Key Learning Outcomes

Critical Thinking

Develop problem-solving skills through scientific inquiry and engineering design challenges.

Collaboration

Work in teams to tackle complex projects, building communication and leadership skills.

Creativity

Apply innovative thinking to design unique solutions and create original projects.
